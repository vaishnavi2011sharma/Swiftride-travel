Best Places to Visit in Goa by Tempo Traveller

Calangute, Baga and Anjuna Beaches

Each beach has its own vibe but all of them have that same thing going on — cheap beer, fresh seafood, and a jet ski ride you probably should not say yes to but definitely will. The tempo traveller drops the group at each beach and waits while everyone explores. No scrambling for transport between beaches during a full day North Goa beach circuit.

Old Goa — Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral

Worth a few hours even if history is not really your thing. The Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral are genuinely impressive up close — the Portuguese left behind some serious architecture and it shows. Parking near Old Goa can be tricky during peak hours. Your driver knows the best spots and entry approach.

Mapusa Market and Anjuna Flea Market

Chaotic, loud, and a little overwhelming — which is exactly why you should go. Pick up some random handicrafts, eat something you cannot quite identify, and soak it all in. The Anjuna Flea Market runs on Wednesdays. A tempo traveller makes this particularly easy — no parking stress, the driver waits while the group shops.

Mandovi River Cruise at Sunset

Low-key one of the best things you can do in Goa. No jet skis, no loud music. Just water, sky, and that specific kind of quiet that is hard to find in Goa after dark. A tempo traveller from your hotel to the jetty and back is the cleanest way to do this stop as part of a longer day tour.

Dudhsagar Waterfalls

Around 60 km from Panaji. One of the tallest waterfalls in India, set inside Mollem National Park. A popular group day trip from Goa — the drive through the forest is part of the experience. Best during monsoon and post-monsoon season. Book the tempo traveller early for Dudhsagar day trips as it is a popular route.

Spice Plantation Tours

Ponda area, around 30 km from Panaji. A favourite for groups that want something genuinely different from the beach circuit. Walk through cardamom, vanilla, and nutmeg plants, watch a cooking demonstration, and eat a proper Goan lunch surrounded by spice gardens. Great half-day outing for family groups and corporate teams.

Sula Vineyards and Winery

A genuinely relaxing afternoon stop especially for corporate groups and mixed-age family trips. Tours, tastings, and views that do not feel like coastal India at all. Book the tempo traveller to drop and pick up — parking at the vineyard is manageable but having one dedicated vehicle for the group makes the visit smoother.

Fort Aguada and Chapora Fort

Two of Goa's most photogenic heritage sites. Fort Aguada is a 17th century Portuguese fort at the northern tip of the Goa coast. Chapora Fort offers a panoramic view of the Chapora River and is famous as a photo spot. Both can be covered as part of a full day North Goa sightseeing circuit by tempo traveller.
