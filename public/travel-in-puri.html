Places to Visit in Puri by Tempo Traveller

Jagannath Temple

One of the four sacred Dhams in India. The most visited religious site in Puri. Non-Hindus are not permitted inside but the outer Singhadwara gate and surrounding area are worth visiting. Early morning darshan recommended for groups to avoid long queues. Driver knows best entry points and waiting areas close to the temple.

Puri Beach

One of the most popular beaches in eastern India. Clean, wide, and easy to access for group visits. Sunrise at Puri Beach is a genuinely special experience for groups visiting early morning. Great for family groups, college trips, and corporate outings combining beach time with temple darshan.

Konark Sun Temple

35 km from Puri. UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most magnificent examples of ancient Indian architecture. Almost every group visiting Puri adds Konark to the itinerary. Half day trip easily managed from Puri by tempo traveller as an afternoon add-on after morning Jagannath darshan.

Chilika Lake

Around 50 km from Puri. Largest coastal lagoon in India with Irrawaddy dolphins, migratory birds, and scenic boat rides across the lake. Popular with nature-loving groups, college trips, and corporate outings. Best visited between November and February.

Raghurajpur Artist Village

Around 14 km from Puri. Traditional Odishan heritage village known for Pattachitra painting, Gotipua dance, and local handicrafts. A unique cultural stop for groups interested in Odisha art and tradition. Easy to include as part of a full day Puri sightseeing tour.

Sakshigopal Temple

Around 20 km from Puri. Important Vaishnava pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Krishna. Popular stop for religious groups combining Jagannath Temple darshan with nearby sacred temple visits on the same day.

Lokanath Temple

One of the oldest Shiva temples in Puri. Located close to Jagannath Temple. Easy to include as part of a full day Puri pilgrimage tour by tempo traveller. An important spiritual stop for Hindu pilgrim groups visiting Puri.

Swargadwar Beach

Considered one of the most sacred cremation sites in India. Spiritually significant for Hindu pilgrimage groups visiting Puri. Located close to the main Puri Beach area and easily covered as part of a full day darshan tour.

Narendra Tank

Large sacred tank in the heart of Puri city. Famous for the Chandana Yatra festival where the deities are taken for a boat ride. Peaceful spot for groups looking for a quieter spiritual experience away from the main temple crowds.

Dhauli Giri

Around 60 km from Puri via Bhubaneswar. Ancient site where Emperor Ashoka converted to Buddhism after the Kalinga War. White Peace Pagoda and rock edicts. Popular heritage and history stop for groups combining Puri with a Bhubaneswar day trip.

Jagannath Temple Darshan Tips for Groups

Arrive Early for Darshan

Temple opens for darshan from early morning. Groups that arrive before 7 AM avoid the longest queues. Driver can be briefed to reach your hotel pickup by 5:30 AM for an early start.

Non-Hindu Entry

Non-Hindu visitors are not permitted inside Jagannath Temple. The outer structure, Singhadwara gate, and surrounding area can be visited. Driver knows exactly where to stop for the best view of the temple exterior.

Combine with Konark

Most groups visiting Jagannath Temple add Konark Sun Temple to the same day itinerary. Konark is 35 km from Puri. Easily managed as an afternoon add-on after morning darshan in Puri.

Rath Yatra Advance Booking

During Rath Yatra, Puri sees massive crowds. Book your tempo traveller at least 3 to 4 weeks in advance during this period. The 20 seater is most popular for large pilgrimage groups during Rath Yatra season.
