Top Attractions in Rameswaram by Tempo Traveller

Ramanathaswamy Temple

Ramanathaswamy Temple is the most famous landmark in Rameswaram and one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlinga temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its magnificent corridors — the longest temple corridors in the world — beautiful pillars, and a deeply peaceful spiritual environment. Pilgrims take a holy bath in the temple's 22 sacred wells before offering prayers. According to the Ramayana, Lord Rama worshipped Lord Shiva here after his victory over Ravana, making it supremely sacred for Hindu pilgrims.

Dhanushkodi Beach

Dhanushkodi is a quiet and scenic place located about 20 km from Rameswaram. It is famous for its untouched beach and the remains of an old town that was destroyed in a cyclone many years ago. Visitors love coming here to enjoy the sea views, the merging of the Bay of Bengal and the Gulf of Mannar, and the peaceful surroundings. A tempo traveller is the most comfortable and practical way to reach Dhanushkodi for your group, as the road passes through a uniquely beautiful coastal landscape.

Agni Theertham

Located near Ramanathaswamy Temple, Agni Theertham is a sacred beach where devotees take a ritual bath before entering the temple. The calm sea and deeply spiritual atmosphere make it a very special place for pilgrims beginning their Rameswaram yatra. It is the first and most important of the 64 theerthams associated with the Ramanathaswamy Temple — a must-visit stop on every Rameswaram sightseeing tour by tempo traveller.

Pamban Bridge

Pamban Bridge is one of the most iconic attractions near Rameswaram — a spectacular cantilever railway bridge that connects Rameswaram Island to mainland India across the Palk Strait. It offers breathtaking views of the sea, and watching trains cross this historic bridge is a unique experience for travelers. The road bridge alongside it offers equally stunning views and is a popular photography stop on every Rameswaram tempo traveller sightseeing package.

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Memorial

This memorial is dedicated to India's beloved former President and Missile Man of India, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, who was born in Rameswaram. The memorial site displays photographs, achievements, and inspiring stories from his extraordinary life — making it a deeply meaningful and motivating place to visit, especially for students and young groups on educational tours from Rameswaram.

Gandhamadhana Parvatham

This small hill is the highest point in Rameswaram and offers a panoramic view of the entire island — a spectacular sight especially at sunrise and sunset. According to belief, it also has the footprints of Lord Rama, which attracts many devotees seeking a connection to the Ramayana. A standard and deeply meaningful stop on every full-day Rameswaram sightseeing tour by tempo traveller.

Arulmigo Ramanatha Temple and Hanuman Temple

Rameswaram is home to several other important temples and sacred shrines beyond the main Jyotirlinga — including the Arulmigo Ramanatha Temple and the Hanuman Temple, both of which are significant stops for pilgrims completing a full darshan circuit of the island. Our experienced local drivers know all the temple routes and parking points, ensuring your group visits every sacred spot without stress or confusion.

Adam's Bridge and Ram Setu View Point

Adam's Bridge — also known as Ram Setu — is a chain of limestone shoals between Rameswaram Island and Sri Lanka, believed by Hindus to be the bridge built by Lord Rama's army to cross to Lanka. The Ram Setu View Point in Rameswaram offers a unique view of this mythological and geological wonder. A fascinating and spiritually charged stop on every Rameswaram darshan tour by tempo traveller.

Best Time to Visit Rameswaram with Family — Plan Your Trip

October to March — Perfect Season for Pilgrimage

The best time to visit Rameswaram is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for temple visits and local sightseeing tours. Cool and comfortable weather ideal for Agni Theertham holy bath, Dhanushkodi beach trips, and long darshan queues at Ramanathaswamy Temple.

Book your tempo traveller in Rameswaram early during this peak season — especially around Maha Shivaratri when demand is highest.

April to June — Summer Visits

Summers are warm and humid in Rameswaram. AC tempo traveller hire in Rameswaram ensures a cool and comfortable ride between all temple stops and sightseeing locations throughout the day. Best to plan early morning darshan and evening beach visits to avoid peak afternoon heat. Short journeys within the island are still very comfortable in a fully AC vehicle.

July to September — Monsoon Season

Monsoon brings occasional heavy rains and rough sea conditions to Rameswaram island. Dhanushkodi road can be affected during heavy rains. Temple visits and indoor darshan remain comfortable throughout the season. Choose experienced local drivers who know safe monsoon routes on the island. Our Rameswaram tempo traveller service ensures safe and reliable travel year round.

Maha Shivaratri is the biggest festival at Ramanathaswamy Temple — attracting lakhs of pilgrims from across India. The entire island sees a massive surge in visitors.
