Places to Visit in Mathura by Tempo Traveller

Shri Krishna Janmasthan

The most noteworthy site in Mathura and believed to be the actual birthplace of Lord Krishna. A live site of reverence where thousands of devotees visit every day to offer prayers. Deep historical and religious significance dating from Dwapar Yuga. An essential first stop on every Mathura pilgrimage tour.

Dwarkadhish Temple Mathura

One of the most visited temples in Mathura, dedicated to Lord Krishna. Built in 1814 by Seth Gokul Das Parikh. Well-known for its elaborate celebrations during Janmashtami and Holi. Beautifully carved pillars and a deeply holy atmosphere. The driver knows the closest parking to the temple entrance.

ISKCON Temple — Krishna Balaram Mandir

Also known as Krishna Balaram Mandir. Established in 1975 by the International Society for Krishna Consciousness. A major attraction for devotees and international visitors from around the world. Known for stunning deity worship, serene environment, and beautifully maintained temple complex.

Vishram Ghat

An exceptionally important place in Mathura, believed to be where Lord Krishna rested after killing Kansa. This ghat on the Yamuna River is a peaceful and calm sacred space, frequently visited for evening aarti. Surrounded by many smaller temples and shrines — a must-visit stop on every Mathura pilgrimage tour.

Prem Mandir, Vrindavan

Located in Vrindavan, 12 km from Mathura. Built by Jagadguru Kripalu Maharaj and renowned for its stunning white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and spectacular evening light show. Beautifully landscaped gardens and fountains make this one of the most photographed temples in the Braj region.

Banke Bihari Temple, Vrindavan

One of the most beloved temples in Vrindavan dedicated to Lord Krishna. Famous for the unique swinging curtain darshan style. The early morning darshan before 8 AM offers the most peaceful experience. Your driver knows the best parking near the temple and the quietest entry approach during peak hours.

Govardhan Parikrama

Located 26 km from Mathura. The 21 km sacred circumambulation circuit around Govardhan Hill is one of the most important pilgrimages in the Braj region. The tempo traveller follows the group and waits at key rest points throughout the parikrama. Starting fare Rs 2,500 for a 12 seater.

Barsana and Nandgaon

Barsana is the birthplace of Radha Rani and home to the famous Lathmar Holi celebration. Nandgaon is the village of Nanda Baba, foster father of Lord Krishna. Both are essential stops on the Braj pilgrimage circuit and are best covered together in one comfortable full day group trip by tempo traveller.
