Best Local Sightseeing Places in Nashik by Tempo Traveller

Trimbakeshwar Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India and one of the most visited religious sites in Maharashtra. Just 28 km from Nashik city. A must-visit for any group doing a local sightseeing trip in Nashik. Best visited early morning before the crowds build up. Driver knows the fastest parking approach.

Panchavati — Ram Kund, Sita Gumpha, Kalaram Temple

The heart of religious Nashik. Ram Kund, Sita Gumpha, and Kalaram Temple. This area carries deep historical and spiritual significance and is one of the most photographed parts of the city. Easy to explore on foot once the tempo traveller drops the group here.

Sula Vineyards

Something completely different and genuinely worth including in the itinerary. Nashik is India's wine capital and Sula is the most well-known vineyard in the region. Tours, tastings, and stunning views of the grape fields. A great afternoon stop especially for corporate groups and mixed-age family trips.

Pandavleni Caves

Ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves dating back over 2,000 years. Situated on a hill just outside the city with good views of Nashik from the top. A short climb but worth it. Usually quiet enough for groups to explore at their own pace — a great heritage stop on any Nashik sightseeing tour.

Ramshej Fort

For groups that enjoy a bit of trekking and history mixed in. The fort sits above Nashik and offers a proper elevated view of the surrounding landscape. Not a very long trek but enough to make it feel like a proper outing. A great add-on for groups visiting on a weekend.

Dugarwadi Waterfall

A popular stop especially during and after monsoon season. Around 35 km from Nashik city. Great for groups that want a mix of nature and sightseeing in the same day trip. Best visited between July and October when the waterfall is at full flow.

Coin Museum and Nashik Old City

For groups that want to understand the city itself. The Nashik Coin Museum, the old city lanes, and the local markets. A slower-paced but genuinely interesting few hours for curious travellers who want something different from the usual temple circuit.

Anjneri Hill — Lord Hanuman Birthplace

Around 22 km from Nashik city. Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Hanuman. A meaningful pilgrimage stop for religious groups visiting Nashik. Short trek to the top with good views. Easily combined with a Trimbakeshwar visit on the same day trip.
