Combine Dwarka to Somnath with These Stops

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga

Just 16 km from Dwarka. Third Jyotirlinga easily added to the same trip before heading toward Somnath. Most groups starting from Dwarka cover Nageshwar darshan first thing in the morning before the long drive south. Makes the trip a 3 Jyotirlinga pilgrimage circuit in one day.

Porbandar

Around halfway between Dwarka and Somnath. Birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi. Kirti Mandir is the most visited heritage site in Porbandar. Easy 30 to 45 minute stop for groups interested in combining pilgrimage with history on the same coastal highway drive.

Madhavpur Beach

Around 60 km before Somnath on the coastal highway. Clean quiet beach with mythological significance as the marriage site of Lord Krishna and Rukmini. A good rest stop for groups on the long drive, especially for groups with children or elderly passengers.

Bhalka Tirth

Just 4 km from Somnath Temple. Sacred site where Lord Krishna was wounded by an arrow before leaving his earthly form. An important stop for pilgrimage groups arriving at Somnath — most drivers will take the group here as a natural addition before or after Somnath Temple darshan.

Sacred Sites in Dwarka by Tempo Traveller

Dwarkadhish Temple

One of the four sacred Char Dhams in India. The most visited religious site in Dwarka, dedicated to Lord Krishna as Dwarkadhish. The five-storey temple with 72 columns is one of the most magnificent temple structures in all of India. A must-visit for every pilgrim arriving in Dwarka.

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga

16 km from Dwarka city. One of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India. The giant statue of Lord Shiva near the temple is one of the most photographed religious landmarks in Gujarat. Usually the first or last stop on every Dwarka pilgrimage itinerary owing to its short distance from the main temple.

Bet Dwarka

An island off the coast of Dwarka, accessible by boat. Believed to be the actual residence of Lord Krishna during his time in Dwarka. The island has several temples and is deeply significant for Vaishnava pilgrims. Ferry service may be restricted during heavy monsoon — confirm at booking if visiting in monsoon.

Rukmini Devi Temple

Around 2 km from Dwarkadhish Temple. Dedicated to Rukmini, the principal queen of Lord Krishna. One of the older temples in Dwarka with beautiful sculptural work. Easily combined with Dwarkadhish darshan on the same morning visit.

Gopi Talav

Sacred lake around 20 km from Dwarka. Associated with the Gopis of Vrindavan who came to meet Lord Krishna after he left for Dwarka. The yellowish soil around the lake is considered sacred and collected by pilgrims. A peaceful and spiritually significant stop on the Panchkroshi circuit.

Dwarka Beach and Gomti Ghat

The confluence of the Gomti River and the Arabian Sea is considered sacred for Hindu pilgrims. Gomti Ghat is a major bathing ghat where pilgrims take a holy dip before entering Dwarkadhish Temple. Dwarka Beach is clean and accessible, and many groups combine a morning beach visit with early temple darshan.
