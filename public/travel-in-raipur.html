Popular Tourist Spots in and Around Raipur by Tempo Traveller

Banjari Mata Temple, Raipur

One of the most well-known temples in Raipur, Chhattisgarh. A divine temple dedicated to Goddess Bangla Mukhi. Deeply significant for devotees from Raipur and surrounding areas. A popular group pilgrimage stop easily covered as part of a local sightseeing day tour by tempo traveller from Raipur.

Ghatarani Waterfall

A popular destination for bathing and natural sightseeing, located in a forested region with Hindu temples, food stalls, and natural attractions. Best visited during and after monsoon season. A favourite half-day or full-day group outing from Raipur by tempo traveller, especially for families and college groups.

Regional Science Centre, Raipur

A well-regarded educational attraction in Chhattisgarh, open every day except Monday. Popular with school groups, college excursions, and families. A good stop to combine with other Raipur city sightseeing on a full day local tour.

Rajim — Triveni Sangam

Around 45 km from Raipur. Known as the Prayagraj of Chhattisgarh. The Triveni Sangam of Mahanadi, Pairy, and Sondur rivers. Famous for the Rajim Kumbh Mela and the ancient Rajim Lochana Temple. One of the most popular pilgrimage routes from Raipur by tempo traveller.

Jagdalpur and Bastar — Chitrakote and Tirathgarh Waterfalls

Around 300 km from Raipur. The Bastar district is one of the most scenic group travel routes in Chhattisgarh — dense forest roads, tribal villages, Chitrakote Waterfall (the Niagara of India), and Tirathgarh Waterfall. A 16 or 20 seater tempo traveller is the practical choice for this route.

Champaran — Birthplace of Sant Vallabhacharya

Around 60 km from Raipur. An important Vaishnava pilgrimage site. Home to the birthplace of Sant Vallabhacharya and the Charbhuja Temple. Popular with religious groups from Raipur covering the full Chhattisgarh pilgrimage circuit by tempo traveller.

Sirpur — Buddhist Heritage Site

Around 85 km from Raipur. One of the most significant Buddhist and Hindu archaeological sites in Central India. Ancient temples, monasteries, and ruins along the Mahanadi. Popular with heritage-focused group tours from Raipur by tempo traveller, especially school and college groups.

Dantewada — Danteshwari Mata Temple

Around 350 km from Raipur in the Bastar region. One of the 52 Shakti Peethas in India. The Danteshwari Temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Chhattisgarh. Popular with large family pilgrimage groups — a 16 or 20 seater tempo traveller from Raipur is the most common booking for this route.

Q11. What is the difference between a tempo traveller and a minibus in Raipur?

A tempo traveller in Raipur typically seats 9 to 20 people and is built for comfortable group travel with better seating, AC, and luggage space than a standard minibus.
